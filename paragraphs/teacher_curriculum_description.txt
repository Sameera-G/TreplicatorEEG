This course provides an introductory overview of nuclear physics, covering the fundamental concepts of the atom's structure, the interactions that govern nuclear stability, and the applications and implications of nuclear phenomena in the world. Through a blend of theoretical understanding and practical implications, students will gain insights into the workings of the atomic nucleus, the forces at play, and the technological advancements stemming from nuclear physics research.

Target Audience:
This curriculum is designed for high school seniors or first-year university students with a basic understanding of physics and chemistry. No prior knowledge of nuclear physics is required.

Course Duration:
This course spans one academic semester, comprising two lectures per week, with each session lasting 90 minutes.
